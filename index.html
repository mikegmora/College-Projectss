About 2PAC

Tupac Amaru Shakur (2PAC) was one of the most influential rappers of all time. He blended powerful storytelling, fearless social commentary, and emotional vulnerability in a way that changed hip-hop forever. Even after his death, his music, poetry, and ideas still impact artists and fans worldwide..

Early Life

  • Born: June 16, 1971, in East Harlem, New York.
  • Real Name: Tupac Amaru Shakur
  • Grew up surrounded by political activism, his family was connected to the Black Panther Party
  • Attended the Baltimore School for the Arts (acting, poetry, and ballet).
  • Known for being extremely intelligent, artistic, and outspoken from a young age
  • His parents divorced when he was 11, and his father was sent to prison for drug trafficking

Music Career

Tupac’s music career exploded because he wasn’t afraid to speak the truth. He talked about poverty, love, struggle, racism, and hope, all with raw honesty. His discography continues to influence modern artists today.

Iconic Albums

  • 2Pacalypse Now (1991) – his debut, focused on political issues.
  • Me Against the World (1995) – considered one of the greatest rap albums ever.
  • All Eyez on Me (1996) – the first double hip-hop album.
  • The Don Killuminati: The 7 Day Theory (1996, as Makaveli).

Most Popular Songs

  • California Love
  • Changes
  • Dear Mama
  • Keep Ya Head Up
  • Ambitionz Az a Ridah

Acting & Poetry

Before rap, Tupac was a poet and actor. He always said acting made him feel free because he could express emotions he never showed in real life.

Movies

  • Juice (1992) – his breakthrough role as Bishop.
  • Poetic Justice (1993) – starring alongside Janet Jackson.
  • Above the Rim (1994).

Poetry

Tupac wrote hundreds of poems, many of them collected into his book, The Rose That Grew from Concrete. His writing shows a more vulnerable, emotional side.

Legacy & Impact

Even decades later, 2PAC remains one of the most important cultural icons in music history. His words sparked conversations about injustice, love, pain, and survival. Modern artists like Kendrick Lamar, J. Cole, and Eminem cite him as a major influence.

  • Inducted into the Rock & Roll Hall of Fame in 2017.
  • Murals of him exist in more than 15 countries.
  • His songs are studied in college classes worldwide.
